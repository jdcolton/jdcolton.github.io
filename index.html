Current Year: 1988 Description: This chart plots the country data from the Lakner-Milanovic (2013) World Panel Income Distribution (LM-WPID) dataset whereby each data point is one country during one "bin year" period. The Y-axis represents the country's lowest decile (Group 1 income) and the X-axis represents the country's highest decile (Group 10 income)
Annotations: A green lines between points indicates there was a decrease in the 10/10 ratio (an inequality metric that's a variation of the 20/20 ratio – measuring the income multiplier of the country’s top decile over the country’s bottom decile) thereby demonstrating a reduction in inequality. A red line indicates the inverse thereby demonstrating an increase in inequality
Tooltip: Hover over a given point to see which country it represents and its 10/10 ratio for that period
Source: https://www.worldbank.org/en/research/brief/World-Panel-Income-Distribution (Note: not all countires have data for every "bin year" period)
